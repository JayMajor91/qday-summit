FAQ

Why should I come to Q-Day? +

Because the conversation happening in that room won't happen anywhere else. Q-Day is not a conference — it's sixty to eighty people who understand the quantum threat to cryptography, in one room, for four hours. No panels for optics. No keynotes for clout. If you're building, funding, or governing systems that rely on public-key cryptography, this is where you need to be.

What is Q-Day and who organises it? +

Q-Day is a half-day invite-only summit on the quantum threat to cryptography, organised by Quantus Network and co-hosted with Network School and Zcash. The event brings together researchers, protocol engineers, institutional investors, and policy representatives to address the post-quantum migration challenge.

Is Q-Day open to the public? +

No. Q-Day is invite-only and attendance is curated. All registrations are reviewed by the organising team. If you work in quantum computing, cryptography, protocol development, institutional capital, or policy — register on Luma and we'll be in touch.

How do I get from Singapore to the venue? +

The venue is at Network School in Johor Bahru, Malaysia — approximately 45 minutes from central Singapore. We recommend taking the MRT to Woodlands and crossing via the Causeway. Taxis and Grab are also widely available from most parts of Singapore.

What is the companion paper? +

The companion paper — 'The State of Quantum Computing: What Crypto Can't Afford to Ignore' — is a technical brief prepared for delegates. It covers quantum hardware timelines, Shor's algorithm, vulnerable cryptographic primitives, and the post-quantum migration path. Over 1,000 copies will be distributed at the event.

How do I sponsor Q-Day? +

Sponsorship tiers start at $2,500 (Community), $5,000 (Silver), and $12,500 (Gold). Use the Become a Sponsor form on this page or reach out directly to the Quantus Network team. Each tier includes brand presence, delegate access, and programme integration.
